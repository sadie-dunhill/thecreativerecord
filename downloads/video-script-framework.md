## The Framework Overview

This is the exact 30-second video ad structure used by 8-figure DTC brands. It works across categories, formats, and platforms.

**Why 30 seconds?**
- Meta rewards completion rates
- 30s is the sweet spot for retention
- Forces clarity and precision
- Easy to adapt to 15s or 60s

---

## The 5-Beat Structure

### Beat 1: The Hook (0-3 seconds)
**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. Create pattern interrupt.

**What to do:**
- Lead with the most compelling thing
- Use visual or audio surprise
- Ask a question they can't ignore
- State a contrarian opinion
- Show the result before the process

**Hook Formulas:**
1. "I used to think [common belief], then I discovered [contrarian truth]"
2. "Stop doing [common practice]"
3. "The real reason [problem] happens"
4. "What [expert] won't tell you about [topic]"
5. "I spent [time/money] so you don't have to"

---

### Beat 2: The Setup (3-8 seconds)
**Purpose:** Establish context. Make the problem relatable.

**What to do:**
- Show you understand their struggle
- Make the problem visceral
- Use specific details (not general complaints)
- Keep it under 5 seconds

**Setup Templates:**
1. "Like you, I [specific struggle]. Every [timeframe], I'd [specific behavior]."
2. "You've probably tried [solution 1], [solution 2], even [extreme solution]."
3. "The problem with [category] is [specific pain point]."

---

### Beat 3: The Discovery (8-15 seconds)
**Purpose:** Introduce your solution. Build credibility.

**What to do:**
- Reveal the product naturally
- Show don't tell
- Use social proof or authority
- Create "aha" moment

**Discovery Formulas:**
1. "Then I found [product]. It's [key differentiator]."
2. "A [authority figure] told me about [mechanism]."
3. "The secret is [unique mechanism]. [Product] has it."

---

### Beat 4: The Proof (15-25 seconds)
**Purpose:** Demonstrate results. Overcome objections.

**What to do:**
- Show transformation (before/after)
- Use specific numbers/metrics
- Address common objections
- Make it visual

**Proof Elements:**
1. Visual transformation (split screen, progression)
2. Specific metrics ("3x more hydrated in 2 weeks")
3. User testimonials (authentic, not polished)
4. Comparison to alternatives

---

### Beat 5: The CTA (25-30 seconds)
**Purpose:** Direct clear action. Remove friction.

**What to do:**
- Tell them exactly what to do
- Create urgency (if real)
- Remove risk (guarantee)
- Show how to start

**CTA Formulas:**
1. "Try [product] risk-free for [timeframe]. Link in bio."
2. "First 100 people get [offer]. Code: [code]."
3. "Start your [trial/refund period]. Link below."

## Complete Example Scripts

### Example 1: Skincare
**Hook:** "I used to think more products meant better skin. Then a dermatologist showed me my medicine cabinet was the problem."

**Setup:** "Seven products every night. $300 a month. And my skin was still dry, flaky, breaking out."

**Discovery:** "She told me: 'You're overwhelming your skin barrier.' One product. Hyaluronic acid plus ceramides. That's it."

**Proof:** "Two weeks and my skin felt different. Balanced. I stopped using everything else. Saved 20 minutes every night."

**CTA:** "Try the barrier repair serum. 60-day guarantee. Link in bio."

### Example 2: Supplements
**Hook:** "Stop drinking protein shakes that taste like chalk. I found one that actually tastes good."

**Setup:** "I've tried them all. The vanilla that tastes like chemicals. I was forcing it down just to hit my protein goals."

**Discovery:** "Then I found Clean Protein. No artificial sweeteners. No bloating. Just real ingredients."

**Proof:** "30 grams of protein. Tastes like a milkshake. I actually look forward to it after workouts."

**CTA:** "Try the sample pack. 5 flavors, $15. Link in bio."

### Example 3: Fashion
**Hook:** "Your $5 t-shirt costs way more than you think. Here's what fast fashion doesn't want you to know."

**Setup:** "I used to buy cheap basics every month. They'd stretch, fade, fall apart. I was spending $500 a year."

**Discovery:** "Then I found Ever Basic. One t-shirt, $48. Organic cotton, made ethically, designed to last 5 years."

**Proof:** "I've worn this shirt 200 times. Still soft, still fits. One purchase replaced 20 fast fashion shirts."

**CTA:** "Build a wardrobe that lasts. Free shipping over $100."

### Example 4: Home
**Hook:** "If you wake up congested every morning, your air is the problem. Not your allergies."

**Setup:** "I tried everything. Nasal sprays, air filters. Still woke up stuffy. Turned out my bedroom air was 5x more polluted than outside."

**Discovery:** "Got the Pure Air 300. Medical-grade HEPA. First night I slept through without waking up congested."

**Proof:** "Two weeks later, I was getting 45 more minutes of deep sleep. We both wake up actually rested."

**CTA:** "Breathe better tonight. 30-day trial."

### Example 5: Fitness
**Hook:** "I canceled my gym membership and got in the best shape of my life. Here's how."

**Setup:** "Gym was 30 minutes away. $80 a month. I'd go for two weeks, then miss a month. Guilt cycle."

**Discovery:** "Found Home Strong. 20-minute workouts. No equipment. I can do them at 6am before the kids wake up."

**Proof:** "Six months. Lost 15 pounds. Gained muscle. Haven't missed a workout in 4 months."

**CTA:** "Start your free 7-day trial."

## Implementation Guide

### Before You Write
1. Define your awareness stage (unaware, problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware)
2. Choose your hook formula
3. Identify your proof points
4. Know your CTA

### Writing Checklist
- [ ] Hook stops the scroll in 3 seconds
- [ ] Setup is relatable and specific
- [ ] Discovery feels natural, not salesy
- [ ] Proof includes numbers or transformation
- [ ] CTA is clear and removes friction
- [ ] Total runtime: 30 seconds (read aloud and time)

### Testing Framework
1. Write 3 versions of the hook
2. Test with 5 people who match your target
3. Ask: "What did you hear in the first 3 seconds?"
4. Iterate based on answers
