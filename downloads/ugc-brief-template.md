## The Problem with Most Briefs

**The brief that fails:**
- "Just make it authentic"
- "Show the product, be yourself"
- "Make it fun and relatable"

**The result:** Generic content, reshoots, wasted budget.

**The brief that works:**
- Specific talking points
- Clear dos and don'ts
- Visual examples
- Defined deliverables

**The result:** Content that converts on first delivery.

## Example Brief: Skincare Brand

**Brand:** Glow Up
**Product:** Vitamin C Serum
**What it does:** Brightens skin and fades dark spots in 4 weeks
**Who it's for:** Women 25-40 with hyperpigmentation
**Differentiator:** 20% vitamin C (competitors use 10-15%)

**Format:** 30-second vertical video
**Hook:** "I stopped using 7 products when I found this one serum"

**Talking points:**
1. Show your morning routine (keep it real)
2. Mention the 20% vitamin C concentration
3. Show the texture and how it absorbs
4. Mention results at 2 weeks vs 4 weeks

**Visuals:**
- Setting: Bathroom, natural morning light
- Show product packaging prominently
- Show applying the serum (close-up)
- No filters or heavy makeup

**Deliverables:**
- 2x 30-second videos (different hooks)
- Raw footage included
- Usage: Paid ads for 6 months

## Quick Brief Checklist

Before sending to creator, confirm:
- [ ] Hook is written out (not just described)
- [ ] Talking points are specific (not vague)
- [ ] Visual references are attached
- [ ] Deliverables are quantified
- [ ] Timeline is realistic
- [ ] Payment terms are clear
- [ ] Usage rights are defined
