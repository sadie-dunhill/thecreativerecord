The Problem

You're spending money on ads you haven't diagnosed

Most brands don't know why a creative failed. They know it had a bad ROAS. They don't know if it was the hook, the pacing, the proof, or the CTA. Without a diagnosis, every new creative is just another guess -- and guesses are expensive.

  • Running the same creative patterns that underperform because you can't identify which specific element is broken and what fix would actually move the needle
  • Spending on iteration without a systematic review process -- you're testing variations of the same broken ad, not testing fixes to diagnosed problems
  • No consistent standard across your creative library, so you can't compare what works, what doesn't, and why -- the knowledge dies with each campaign
What's Inside

25 scored criteria. One clear action plan.

Score each of the 25 criteria on a 1-5 scale. Total your score. Use the interpretation guide and quick wins to fix what's broken this week.

  • 25-Point Scoring Checklist — 5 sections (Hook, Pacing, Clarity, Proof, CTA), 5 criteria each, 1-5 score per point, /125 total

  • Red Flag Checklist — 6 critical issues (fix now), 6 major issues (fix soon), and 4 minor issues (optimize later) with clear severity tiers
  • Priority Action Matrix — four-quadrant system: Fix Now, Test Next, Optimize Later, and Monitor -- so you always know what to work on first
  • Section-Specific Quick Wins — if your hook scores low, do this; if your proof scores low, do this -- immediate, actionable fixes for every weak area
  • Full Audit Walkthrough Example — a complete 30-second skincare video audited section by section, so you see exactly how to use the checklist

FAQ

Common questions

Does this work for static ads as well as video?
Yes. The checklist is written for video but the five sections -- hook, pacing, clarity, proof, CTA -- apply equally to static ads. "Pacing" becomes "information hierarchy" for statics, but the criteria translate directly.
How long does it actually take to use?
About 20 minutes for a thorough audit. A quick pre-flight review of just the hook section and CTA section takes 5 minutes. The full audit is designed for your top spenders and anything before a new campaign launch.
Can I build this into my creative review process?
That's exactly how it's designed to be used. The Notion template version is formatted as a database so you can score and store audits for every creative -- giving you a searchable record of what worked and what didn't.
What if my creative scores low -- then what?
The priority action matrix tells you exactly what to do. Scores of 1-2 go in the "Fix Now" quadrant with section-specific quick wins. Scores of 2-3 go to "Test Next." The checklist is a diagnosis, not just a score -- every section comes with repair guidance.
