Video Script Framework

🎬
What It Helps You Do

Write a 30-second video ad script for any product.

Uses a 5-beat structure that works across every product category. No advertising experience needed.
When to Use It

Any time you need a video ad and don't know where to start.

Works for Facebook/Instagram ads, TikTok, YouTube, and any short promotional video.
3-Step Process
1
Read an example (5 min)Open EXAMPLES/example-skincare-brand.md. Read the whole script. You'll see how all 5 beats flow together before writing your own.
2
Fill in your product details (10 min)Open TEMPLATES/script-template-blank.md. Answer the 8 questions at the top. Replace every [BRACKET] with your own information.
3
Write one beat at a time, then read it aloud (20 min)Write Beat 1 (hook) through Beat 5 (CTA). Time your final script: aim for 28–32 seconds out loud.
Example — Skincare Brand Script (29 seconds)
Hook: "If you're still breaking out in your 30s — this is why."  |  Problem: "Adult acne is hormonal. Most products treat you like you're 16."  |  Product: "Clear Skin Co. uses willow bark and niacinamide — gentle ingredients that work with your cycle."  |  Proof: "847 reviews, 4.8 stars. First clear month in years."  |  CTA: "Visit clearskinco.com — code FIRST20 for 20% off."

Hook Bank Template

🎣
What It Helps You Do

Find the perfect first line for any video ad.

100+ proven hooks organized by awareness stage. Find your section, pick a hook, swap in your details.
When to Use It

Every time you write a new video ad and need a strong opening line.

The hook is the most important part of any ad. Use this before every video shoot.
3-Step Process
1
Find your awareness stage (2 min)Does your customer know they have a problem? Yes → Problem-Aware section. No → Unaware. Tried other products → Solution-Aware.
2
Read your section and pick 3 hooks (5 min)Open hook-bank-template.md and go to your section. When a hook makes you think "that's my customer" — that's the one. Pick 3 to test.
3
Replace the brackets with specific details (5 min)Swap every [BRACKET] with your real product, result, or timeframe. Specific beats vague — "17 lbs" beats "lose weight."
3 Examples by Category
Skincare: "Still breaking out in your 30s? Here's why your cleanser is making it worse."  |  Supplement: "I was sleeping 8 hours and still exhausted. Turns out it wasn't a sleep problem."  |  Fitness: "The 12-minute routine I've done every morning for 2 years."

UGC Brief Template

🎥
What It Helps You Do

Write a clear brief for any content creator.

Tell creators exactly what to say and show so they deliver exactly what you need — on the first try.
When to Use It

Every time you hire a creator or influencer to make a video about your product.

Also works when briefing employees, friends, or yourself to film a video.
3-Step Process
1
Open the blank brief and fill in Section 1 (5 min)Open TEMPLATES/ugc-brief-blank.md. Answer the 5 product overview questions. This is the foundation everything else builds on.
2
Write 4 talking points as complete sentences (10 min)Section 2 is most important. Write each point as a full sentence — not a topic, a sentence the creator could almost say directly.
3
Send with one instruction (2 min)Send the brief and say: "Please confirm you've read all of this before filming." That single line prevents 90% of revision requests.
Example Talking Points — Skincare Brand
1. "I've struggled with adult acne for years and nothing was working — always breaking out right before my period."  |  2. "Clear Skin Co. uses gentle ingredients that work with your hormonal cycle — no harsh chemicals."  |  3. "Within 6 weeks, my clearest skin in years. No peeling, no dryness."  |  4. "If you're an adult still dealing with breakouts, this is different from anything else I've tried."

Creative Audit Checklist

✅
What It Helps You Do

Score any video ad on 25 points and know what to fix.

Takes 20 minutes. Gives you a number between 0–125 and tells you exactly what's working and what's broken.
When to Use It

Before spending money on a new ad, or when an existing ad stops working.

Use it on your best performers too — even good ads have something to improve.
3-Step Process
1
Watch your ad twice before scoring (5 min)First watch: just watch. Second watch: take notes on what feels weak. Now open TEMPLATES/audit-scorecard-blank.md.
2
Score all 25 points 1–5 (15 min)5 sections: Hook, Problem, Product, Proof, CTA. Score each point while watching. When unsure, give a 3. Total score = 5 sections × 5 points × 5 max = 125.
3
Fix the lowest section first (immediately)100–125 = scale it. 75–99 = one section to fix. 50–74 = major revision. Below 50 = rebuild from scratch using the Video Script Framework.
Example Score — Supplement Brand Ad
Hook: 18/25 (good but not specific enough)  |  Problem: 21/25 (strong)  |  Product: 18/25 (missing differentiator)  |  Proof: 21/25 (strong)  |  CTA: 20/25 (no urgency)  |  Total: 98/125 — Good. Fix: add differentiator in Beat 3.

Competitor Analysis Framework

🔍
What It Helps You Do

See what competitors are running — and find the gaps they're missing.

Uses the free Meta Ad Library. No advertising experience needed. Takes 45 minutes total.
When to Use It

Before creating new ads, or when your existing ads feel like everyone else's.

Do this quarterly to stay ahead of what's getting saturated in your market.
3-Step Process
1
Go to facebook.com/ads/library (free) (10 min)Search 3–5 competitor brand names. Look at their active ads. Write down the hook (first line) of every ad running 30+ days.
2
Fill in the competitor tracker (15 min)Open TEMPLATES/competitor-tracker-blank.md. One row per ad. Track: hook, claim, proof, format, how long it's been running.
3
Find what nobody is doing (10 min)What angle does every competitor use? That's the crowded lane. What angle is nobody using? What problem is nobody talking about? That's your gap.
Example Gap — Adult Acne Category
Everyone is saying: "Say goodbye to acne," "Dermatologist recommended," "Natural ingredients" — no differentiation.  |  Nobody is saying: "Adult hormonal acne is different from teenage acne — you need a different solution."  |  The ad: "I'm 34 and I break out worse now than in high school. Turns out that's a completely different problem."
