## Awareness Stage Guide

### Problem-Aware
Your audience knows they have a problem, but doesn't know solutions exist.

**Signals:** They complain about the problem. They Google symptoms. They try DIY fixes.

**Hook approach:** Agitate the problem. Make them feel it.

### Solution-Aware
Your audience knows solutions exist, but hasn't found the right one.

**Signals:** They compare options. They read reviews. They're solution-shopping.

**Hook approach:** Differentiate your solution. Why yours, not theirs?

### Product-Aware
Your audience knows your product exists, but hasn't bought yet.

**Signals:** They've visited your site. They follow you on social. They're considering.

**Hook approach:** Overcome objections. Create urgency. Make it easy to say yes.

### Most-Aware
Your audience knows everything. They just need a reason to buy now.

**Signals:** They're on your email list. They've added to cart. They're close.

**Hook approach:** Deal, bonus, urgency. Remove final friction.

## Hook Testing Framework

### Step 1: Select 3 Hooks
- Pick one from your awareness stage
- Adapt to your product using formulas above
- Write 3 variations

### Step 2: Test on Platform
- Run each hook as 3-second video start
- Same video, different hooks
- $50 budget each for 24 hours

### Step 3: Measure
- Hook rate (3-second view rate)
- Thumb-stop ratio
- Cost per 3-second view

### Step 4: Scale Winner
- Take winning hook
- Test new variations on same structure
- Iterate weekly

## Hook Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Too generic** - "Amazing product you'll love" (says nothing)
2. **Too clever** - Puns and wordplay don't stop scrolls
3. **Too vague** - "Transform your life" (how specifically?)
4. **Too long** - If it takes 5 seconds to say, it's too long
5. **No pattern interrupt** - If it looks like every other ad, it fails
