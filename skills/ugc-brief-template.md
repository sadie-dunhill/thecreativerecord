## The Problem with Most Briefs

**The brief that fails:**
- "Just make it authentic"
- "Show the product, be yourself"
- "Make it fun and relatable"

**The result:**
- Generic content that looks like every other ad
- Creators guessing what you want
- Reshoots, delays, disappointment
- Wasted budget on unusable content

**The brief that works:**
- Specific talking points (not vague direction)
- Clear dos and don'ts
- Visual examples and references
- Defined deliverables and specs

**The result:**
- Content that converts on first delivery
- Creators who feel confident and supported
- No reshoots needed
- Content you can actually use

## Example Briefs

### Example 1: Skincare Brand

**Brand:** Glow Up
**Product:** Vitamin C Serum

**What it does:** Brightens skin and fades dark spots in 4 weeks

**Who it's for:** Women 25-40 dealing with hyperpigmentation and dull skin

**Key differentiator:** 20% vitamin C (most competitors use 10-15%)

**Brand voice:** Professional, educational, empowering

**Format:** 30-second vertical video

**Hook requirement:** "I stopped using 7 products when I found this one serum"

**Talking points:**
1. Show your morning routine (keep it real, not polished)
2. Mention the 20% vitamin C concentration
3. Share one specific result you've seen (brightness, texture, spots)
4. Show how it absorbs (not greasy)
5. Mention the 60-day guarantee

**Required elements:**
- Product packaging in frame
- Serum texture close-up
- Application on face
- Natural lighting

**No-nos:**
- No filter/beauty mode
- No other skincare brands visible
- Don't say "miracle" or "instant results"

**Personal story prompt:** "Talk about your previous skincare routine vs. now with just this serum"

**Setting:** Bathroom or bedroom, natural morning light

**Deliverables:**
- 2x 30-second videos
- 3x raw clips (optional angles)
- Written caption (brand will provide)
- Organic + paid usage rights

**Timeline:** 7 days from brief acceptance

### Example 2: Fitness App

**Brand:** Home Strong
**Product:** Fitness app subscription

**What it does:** 20-minute home workouts, no equipment needed

**Who it's for:** Busy people who want to get fit but can't get to the gym

**Key differentiator:** Workouts designed by physical therapists (injury prevention)

**Brand voice:** Motivating, accessible, no BS

**Format:** 60-second video showing workout + results

**Hook requirement:** "I canceled my gym membership and got stronger at home"

**Talking points:**
1. Show your workout space (keep it real - doesn't need to be fancy)
2. Do 2-3 exercises from the app (we'll provide access)
3. Mention the 20-minute length
4. Share how long you've been using it and one result
5. Mention it's designed by PTs (safer than random YouTube workouts)

**Required elements:**
- App interface visible on phone
- You doing the exercises
- Sweat/real effort visible
- Home setting (not gym)

**No-nos:**
- No before/after body shots
- No weight loss claims
- No other fitness apps visible

**Personal story prompt:** "Talk about why you couldn't stick with the gym and how this changed that"

**Setting:** Living room, bedroom, backyard - wherever you actually work out

**Deliverables:**
- 1x 60-second main video
- 2x 15-second clips for ads
- Raw footage of 3 exercises
- Usage: Paid ads only (1 year)

**Timeline:** 10 days from app access

### Example 3: Meal Delivery Service

**Brand:** Fresh Plate
**Product:** Meal kit subscription

**What it does:** Delivers pre-portioned ingredients for home-cooked meals

**Who it's for:** Busy professionals who want to cook but don't have time to plan/shop

**Key differentiator:** 15-minute meals (not 45-minute recipes)

**Brand voice:** Practical, aspirational but achievable, warm

**Format:** 30-second unboxing + cook + taste

**Hook requirement:** "From box to plate in 15 minutes. Here's how."

**Talking points:**
1. Unboxing moment (show excitement)
2. Show the recipe card
3. Time-lapse or quick cuts of cooking
4. Final plated dish
5. Taste reaction (authentic, not overdone)

**Required elements:**
- Branded box opening
- Recipe card visible
- Ingredients spread out
- Cooking process
- Finished dish
- You eating/enjoying

**No-nos:**
- No competing meal kits visible
- Don't claim it's "cheaper than grocery shopping" (depends)
- No negative comments about cooking

**Personal story prompt:** "Talk about your usual dinner routine vs. using Fresh Plate"

**Setting:** Kitchen, natural or good artificial light

**Deliverables:**
- 1x 30-second edited video
- 3-5 raw clips of key moments
- Written caption (you write, we approve)
- Usage: Organic + paid (6 months)

**Timeline:** 5 days from box delivery

## Content Review Checklist

When reviewing creator submissions, check:

**Technical:**
- [ ] Correct format and dimensions
- [ ] Good lighting and audio
- [ ] No technical issues (blur, focus, etc.)

**Content:**
- [ ] Hook is strong (first 3 seconds)
- [ ] Key message is clear
- [ ] CTA is present
- [ ] Required elements are included
- [ ] No no-nos present

**Brand alignment:**
- [ ] Voice/tone matches brand
- [ ] Visual style fits guidelines
- [ ] Creator feels authentic to brand

**Conversion potential:**
- [ ] Would this stop a scroll?
- [ ] Is the value proposition clear?
- [ ] Would this drive action?

## Red Flags (When to Request Reshoots)

**Automatic reshoot:**
- Missing required elements
- Wrong product shown
- Competitor products visible
- Technical issues (can't be fixed in editing)
- Off-brand messaging

**Fix in editing:**
- Pacing too slow/fast
- Lighting could be better
- Minor framing issues
- Add text overlay

**Approve with notes:**
- Could be stronger but usable
- Minor tweaks suggested
- Good for testing but optimize next round

## Tips for Getting Great Content Back

1. **Over-communicate** - Better to give too much direction than too little
2. **Show examples** - Link to 2-3 videos in the style you want
3. **Be realistic** - Don't expect Hollywood production from $100 UGC
4. **Give creative freedom** - Within your constraints, let them be themselves
5. **Build relationships** - Good creators are worth keeping
6. **Pay fairly** - $50-200 per video depending on creator tier and usage rights
7. **Review quickly** - Get feedback to them within 48 hours
8. **Be specific with edits** - "Make it more energetic" vs "Add more hand gestures and smile bigger"

## Next Steps

1. Fill out the brief template for your product
2. Find 3-5 creators who fit your target audience
3. Send outreach emails with brief attached
4. Review and provide feedback on submissions
5. Approve, pay, and start using the content

**Remember:** A great brief gets you 80% of the way there. The other 20% is finding the right creator and building a relationship.
