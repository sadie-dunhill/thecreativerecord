# Video Script Framework
## The 30-Second Structure That Converts

**Version 1.0** | The Creative Record | March 2026

## The Framework Overview

This is the exact 30-second video ad structure used by 8-figure DTC brands. It works across categories, formats, and platforms. Use it as your foundation, then adapt to your brand voice.

**Why 30 seconds?**
- Meta rewards completion rates
- 30s is the sweet spot for retention
- Forces clarity and precision
- Easy to adapt to 15s or 60s

---

## The 5-Beat Structure

### Beat 1: The Hook (0-3 seconds)
**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. Create pattern interrupt.

**What to do:**
- Lead with the most compelling thing
- Use visual or audio surprise
- Ask a question they can't ignore
- State a contrarian opinion
- Show the result before the process

**Hook Formulas:**
1. "I used to think [common belief], then I discovered [contrarian truth]"
2. "Stop doing [common practice]"
3. "The real reason [problem] happens"
4. "What [expert] won't tell you about [topic]"
5. "I spent [time/money] so you don't have to"

**Example:**
> "I used to spend $300 on skincare that didn't work. Then a dermatologist told me the truth about moisturizers." (3 seconds)

---

### Beat 2: The Setup (3-8 seconds)
**Purpose:** Establish context. Make the problem relatable.

**What to do:**
- Show you understand their struggle
- Make the problem visceral
- Use specific details (not general complaints)
- Keep it under 5 seconds

**Setup Templates:**
1. "Like you, I [specific struggle]. Every [timeframe], I'd [specific behavior]."
2. "You've probably tried [solution 1], [solution 2], even [extreme solution]."
3. "The problem with [category] is [specific pain point]."

**Example:**
> "For years, I was layering 7 products every night. Serums, toners, masks. My bathroom looked like a Sephora. And my skin? Still dry." (5 seconds)

---

### Beat 3: The Discovery (8-15 seconds)
**Purpose:** Introduce your solution. Build credibility.

**What to do:**
- Reveal the product naturally
- Show don't tell
- Use social proof or authority
- Create "aha" moment

**Discovery Formulas:**
1. "Then I found [product]. It's [key differentiator]."
2. "A [authority figure] told me about [mechanism]."
3. "The secret is [unique mechanism]. [Product] has it."

**Example:**
> "Then my dermatologist told me: 'You're over-moisturizing.' She recommended one product with hyaluronic acid and ceramides. That's it. Just this." (7 seconds)

---

### Beat 4: The Proof (15-25 seconds)
**Purpose:** Demonstrate results. Overcome objections.

**What to do:**
- Show transformation (before/after)
- Use specific numbers/metrics
- Address common objections
- Make it visual

**Proof Elements:**
1. Visual transformation (split screen, progression)
2. Specific metrics ("3x more hydrated in 2 weeks")
3. User testimonials (authentic, not polished)
4. Comparison to alternatives

**Example:**
> "Two weeks in, my skin felt different. Not greasy, not tight. Just... balanced. I stopped using everything else. My routine went from 7 products to 1. Saved me 20 minutes every night." (10 seconds)

---

### Beat 5: The CTA (25-30 seconds)
**Purpose:** Direct clear action. Remove friction.

**What to do:**
- Tell them exactly what to do
- Create urgency (if real)
- Remove risk (guarantee)
- Show how to start

**CTA Formulas:**
1. "Try [product] risk-free for [timeframe]. Link in bio."
2. "First 100 people get [offer]. Code: [code]."
3. "Start your [trial/refund period]. Link below."

**Example:**
> "If you're tired of complicated routines that don't work, try this. 60-day guarantee. Link in bio to get 20% off your first order." (5 seconds)

## Complete Example Scripts

### Example 1: Skincare (Hydrating Serum)

**Hook (0-3s):** "I used to think more products meant better skin. Then a dermatologist showed me my medicine cabinet was the problem."

**Setup (3-8s):** "Seven products every night. $300 a month. And my skin was still dry, flaky, breaking out. I was doing everything right and getting nowhere."

**Discovery (8-15s):** "She told me: 'You're overwhelming your skin barrier.' One product. Hyaluronic acid plus ceramides. That's it."

**Proof (15-25s):** "Two weeks. Just two weeks and my skin felt different. Balanced. Not greasy, not tight. I stopped using everything else. Saved 20 minutes every night and $200 a month."

**CTA (25-30s):** "Try the barrier repair serum. 60-day guarantee. Link in bio for 20% off."

**Total: 30 seconds**

---

### Example 2: Supplements (Protein Powder)

**Hook (0-3s):** "Stop drinking protein shakes that taste like chalk. I found one that actually tastes good."

**Setup (3-8s):** "I've tried them all. The vanilla that tastes like chemicals. The chocolate that sits heavy. I was forcing it down just to hit my protein goals."

**Discovery (8-15s):** "Then I found Clean Protein. No artificial sweeteners. No bloating. Just real ingredients that mix smooth and taste like dessert."

**Proof (15-25s):** "30 grams of protein. Tastes like a milkshake. I actually look forward to it after workouts. My digestion improved. No more post-shake bloat."

**CTA (25-30s):** "Try the sample pack. 5 flavors, $15. Link in bio."

**Total: 30 seconds**

### Example 3: Fashion (Sustainable Basics)

**Hook (0-3s):** "Your $5 t-shirt costs way more than you think. Here's what fast fashion doesn't want you to know."

**Setup (3-8s):** "I used to buy cheap basics every month. They'd stretch, fade, fall apart. I was spending $500 a year on clothes that looked like trash after three washes."

**Discovery (8-15s):** "Then I found Ever Basic. One t-shirt, $48. Organic cotton, made ethically, designed to last 5 years. Cost per wear: way less than fast fashion."

**Proof (15-25s):** "I've worn this shirt 200 times. Still soft, still fits, still looks new. One purchase replaced 20 fast fashion shirts. Better for my wallet, better for the planet."

**CTA (25-30s):** "Build a wardrobe that lasts. Free shipping over $100. Link below."

**Total: 30 seconds**

### Example 4: Home (Air Purifier)

**Hook (0-3s):** "If you wake up congested every morning, your air is the problem. Not your allergies."

**Setup (3-8s):** "I tried everything. Nasal sprays, air filters, changing my sheets. Still woke up stuffy. Turned out the air in my bedroom was 5x more polluted than outside."

**Discovery (8-15s):** "Got the Pure Air 300. Medical-grade HEPA. Whisper quiet. Covers 500 square feet. First night I slept through without waking up congested."

**Proof (15-25s):** "Two weeks later, my sleep tracker showed I was getting 45 more minutes of deep sleep. My partner stopped snoring. We both wake up actually rested."

**CTA (25-30s):** "Breathe better tonight. 30-day trial, free returns. Link in bio."

**Total: 30 seconds**

### Example 5: Fitness (Home Workout Program)

**Hook (0-3s):** "I canceled my gym membership and got in the best shape of my life. Here's how."

**Setup (3-8s):** "Gym was 30 minutes away. $80 a month. I'd go for two weeks, then life would happen. Miss a day, miss a week, miss a month. Guilt cycle."

**CTA (25-30s):** "Start your free 7-day trial. Link below. Cancel anytime."

**Total: 30 seconds**

## Adaptation Guide

### For 15-Second Ads
- Hook: 0-2s
- Setup: 2-5s (condensed)
- Discovery + Proof: 5-12s (combined)
- CTA: 12-15s

### For 60-Second Ads
- Hook: 0-3s
- Setup: 3-10s (expand the struggle)
- Discovery: 10-20s (add mechanism explanation)
- Proof: 20-50s (multiple proof points)
- CTA: 50-60s (multiple CTAs, urgency)

### For Different Platforms

**TikTok/Reels:**
- Faster pacing
- More visual hooks
- Text overlays for sound-off viewing
- Trending audio integration

**Meta Feed:**
- Front-load the value proposition
- Use captions (85% watch without sound)
- Product showcase in first 3 seconds

**YouTube Pre-roll:**
- Can't skip first 5 seconds, so make it count
- Longer setup works (captive audience)
- More detailed proof

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Starting with the product** - Lead with the problem, not the solution
2. **Being too polished** - Authentic > professional for most DTC
3. **Skipping the setup** - If they don't feel the pain, they won't care about the solution
4. **Vague claims** - "Better" is meaningless. "3x more" is specific.
5. **Weak CTAs** - "Check us out" doesn't work. "Link in bio for 20% off" does.
