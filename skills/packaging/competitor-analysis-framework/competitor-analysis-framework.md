# Competitor Analysis Framework
## How to Spy on Competitors (Ethically and Effectively)

**Version 1.0** | The Creative Record | March 2026

---

## Why Competitor Analysis Matters

**The wrong way:**
- Copy their exact hooks
- Mimic their visual style
- Chase every trend they start
- Assume what's working for them will work for you

**The right way:**
- Understand what they're testing
- Learn from their positioning
- Find gaps they're missing
- Build your own advantage

**The goal:** Intelligence, not imitation.

---

## What to Analyze

### 1. Creative Volume & Frequency

**What to track:**
- How many new ads per week/month?
- What formats are they prioritizing?
- Seasonal patterns or always-on?

**Why it matters:**
- High volume = aggressive testing budget
- Format priority = what's working for them
- Frequency = their comfort zone for creative refresh

**How to find it:**
- Meta Ad Library: Search brand name, filter by active ads
- TikTok Creative Center: Search brand, view recent ads
- Manual tracking: Screenshot and date every new ad you see

**Track in spreadsheet:**
| Date | Brand | Format | Hook | CTA | Notes |
|------|-------|--------|------|-----|-------|

---

### 2. Creative Lifespan

**What to track:**
- How long do they run the same ad?
- When do they refresh creative?
- Do they iterate on winners or start fresh?

**Why it matters:**
- Long lifespan = strong performer
- Short lifespan = testing aggressively
- Iteration pattern = their optimization strategy

**How to track:**
- Screenshot ads weekly
- Note first seen date and last seen date
- Calculate lifespan in days

**Benchmarks:**
- 7-14 days: Testing phase
- 15-30 days: Promising
- 30+ days: Strong performer
- 60+ days: Clear winner

---

### 3. Hook Patterns & Messaging

**What to track:**
- What hooks do they use most?
- What awareness stage are they targeting?
- What pain points do they emphasize?
- What benefits do they lead with?

**Why it matters:**
- Hook patterns = what's resonating
- Awareness stage = who they're targeting
- Pain points = customer research insights
- Benefits = their value proposition testing

**Categorize hooks by type:**
- Problem-aware (agitating pain)
- Solution-aware (differentiating)
- Product-aware (social proof)
- Most-aware (deals/urgency)

**Track in matrix:**
| Hook Type | Example | Frequency | Your Opportunity |
|-----------|---------|-----------|------------------|

---

### 4. Visual Style & Production

**What to track:**
- UGC vs. polished production
- Studio vs. lifestyle settings
- Talent types (age, style, vibe)
- Color palette and aesthetic

**Why it matters:**
- Production level = their budget and strategy
- Setting choices = what feels authentic to their audience
- Talent = who they're trying to reach
- Aesthetic = brand positioning

**Questions to ask:**
- Are they trending toward more or less polished?
- What settings feel most "them"?
- Who's in their ads? (Demographics, style)
- How has their visual style evolved?

---

### 5. Offers & Positioning

**What to track:**
- What offers do they run?
- How do they position price?
- What guarantees or risk reversals?
- Premium vs. value positioning?

**Why it matters:**
- Offer patterns = what's converting
- Positioning = their brand strategy
- Price framing = how they overcome cost objections
- Guarantees = what reduces friction

**Track offers:**
| Offer Type | Frequency | Duration | Your Response |
|------------|-----------|----------|---------------|

## Where to Find Competitor Ads

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
**Meta Ad Library:**
1. Go to facebook.com/ads/library
2. Search competitor brand name
3. Filter by country and active status
4. View all active ads

**What you can see:**
- All active ads
- When each ad started running
- Platform placement
- Approximate spend (sometimes)

**Pro tip:** Check weekly to see what's new and what's still running

---

### TikTok
**TikTok Creative Center:**
1. Go to ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter
2. Search competitor brand
3. View top ads and recent ads

**What you can see:**
- Top performing ads in category
- Recent ads from specific brands
- Engagement metrics (sometimes)

**TikTok search:**
- Search brand name + "ad" or "sponsored"
- Check hashtags they're using
- See what creators are posting about them

---

### YouTube
**YouTube Ads:**
- Watch for their pre-roll ads
- Screenshot and document
- Note targeting (what videos you see them on)

**VidIQ or TubeBuddy:**
- Can sometimes see competitor video performance
- Check their channel content strategy

---

### Google
**Google Ads Transparency:**
- Search for their brand
- Check Shopping ads
- Note search ad copy

**SpyFu or SEMrush:**
- See their paid keywords
- Estimate ad spend
- View ad copy variations

---

### Instagram/Twitter Organic
**Organic content reveals:**
- Brand voice and personality
- Content themes
- Community engagement
- Behind-the-scenes

**What to track:**
- Posting frequency
- Content types (educational, promotional, community)
- Engagement rates
- What gets the most response

|------------|----------------|------------------------|---------------------|

### Aspirational Competitors (Bigger brands you want to be like)

| Brand | What They Do Well | How to Apply It |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Brand F | Storytelling | Adapt narrative structure |

## Analysis Framework: The 4 Quadrants

Plot competitors on this matrix:

```
                    HIGH PRODUCTION
                           |
         Premium      |    Luxury
         Positioning  |    Positioning
         (High $$$)   |    (Very High $$$)
                           |
LOW CREATIVE  ------------+------------  HIGH CREATIVE
INNOVATION                |              INNOVATION
         Budget        |    Challenger
         Positioning  |    Positioning
         (Low $$)     |    (Medium $$$)
                           |
                    LOW PRODUCTION
```

**Where do you want to be?**
- Most DTC brands aim for "Challenger"
- Premium positioning with creative innovation
- Not the cheapest, not the most expensive
- Just the best value with strongest creative

**Gap analysis:**
- Is there an underserved quadrant?
- Can you own a positioning no one else has?
- What's the white space?

## What to Do With Intelligence

### 1. Learn, Don't Copy

**Good learning:**
- "They're using problem-aware hooks heavily. Our audience might need more education."
- "Their UGC looks authentic and performs well. We should test creator content."
- "They lead with price. Maybe we should test value-focused messaging."

**Bad copying:**
- Using their exact hook
- Mimicking their visual style exactly
- Running the same offer

**Rule:** Understand why it works, then do it your way.

---

### 2. Find the Gap

**Questions to ask:**
- What angle aren't they using?
- What audience segment are they ignoring?
- What format haven't they tried?
- What benefit aren't they emphasizing?

**Example:**
- Competitors all focus on speed/convenience
- Gap: Quality/craft positioning
- Opportunity: Own the "made with care" angle

---

### 3. Anticipate Their Moves

**If you see them testing:**
- New hooks → They're trying to find new angles
- New formats → Platform shift or format expansion
- Aggressive offers → Facing conversion challenges
- Influencer push → Building social proof

**Your response:**
- Don't react immediately
- Watch for 2-3 weeks to see what sticks
- Then decide if you need to counter-position

---

### 4. Benchmark Your Performance

**Track vs. competitors:**
- Your hook rate vs. industry average
- Your CPM vs. theirs (if you can estimate)
- Your creative lifespan vs. theirs
- Your messaging evolution vs. theirs

**Goal:** Be in top 20% of your competitive set

## Weekly Competitor Review Process

**15 minutes every Monday:**

1. **Check Meta Ad Library (5 min)**
   - What's new from top 3 competitors?
   - What's still running from last week?
   - Screenshot anything interesting

2. **TikTok Creative Center (5 min)**
   - Category trends
   - Competitor new releases
   - Top performers in your space

3. **Update tracking spreadsheet (5 min)**
   - Log new ads
   - Note any patterns
   - Flag anything for deeper analysis

**Monthly deep dive (1 hour):**
- Full competitive matrix update
- Analyze 3 competitor ads in detail
- Identify 2-3 opportunities/gaps
- Brief creative team on findings

## Red Flags (When to Worry)

**Competitor is:**
- Running way more creative than you (testing budget you can't match)
- Finding hooks that really resonate (catch up fast)
- Aggressively expanding (market share grab)
- Copying you (flattery or threat?)

**Your response:**
- Don't panic
- Double down on what makes you unique
- Increase testing velocity
- Protect your winning creative

- When you have 3+ major competitors
- When analysis takes >2 hours/week manually

## The Ethics of Competitive Intelligence

**Do:**
- Analyze publicly visible ads
- Learn from patterns
- Find inspiration
- Identify gaps

**Don't:**
- Click competitor ads repeatedly (costs them money)
- Use their exact copy or creative
- Spread misinformation about them
- Violate platform terms of service

**Golden rule:** Compete on value, not on sabotage.
