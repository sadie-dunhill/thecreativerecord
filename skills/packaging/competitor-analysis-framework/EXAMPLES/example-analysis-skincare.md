## Pattern Finder

**What hooks are EVERYONE using?**
→ "Say goodbye to," "Finally," "Fed up with," "Say hello to" — all variations on the same hope/frustration formula. Zero differentiation.

**What claims does EVERYONE make?**
→ "Clear skin in X days," "Dermatologist recommended," "Natural ingredients." Every brand claims the same things with different words.

**What formats does EVERYONE use?**
→ Before/after photos and customer testimonials. Very few brands use creator UGC.

**What problem does NOBODY talk about?**
→ Nobody talks about *adult hormonal acne specifically.* Every brand treats "acne" as one thing. Nobody acknowledges that adult acne (especially hormonal, jawline, cycle-related) is different from teenage acne.

**What emotional angle does NOBODY use?**
→ Nobody talks about the embarrassment of being an adult woman who still deals with breakouts. Nobody validates the specific frustration of "I'm not a teenager, why is this still happening?"

**Who does NOBODY target specifically?**
→ Women 28–42 with hormonal/cycle-related acne. Everyone is targeting "people with acne" or "teens."

---

## My Gap (The Opportunity)

**The gap:** Nobody is speaking to adult women with hormonal breakouts as a specific identity. Every competitor assumes their customer is a teenager or a generic "acne sufferer."

**My ad idea that takes this gap:**

Hook: "I'm 34 and I break out worse now than I did in high school. Turns out that's a completely different problem."

This acknowledges the specific frustration (adult acne in your 30s), names the insight (it's a different problem), and sets up a product that understands the difference. No competitor is running this angle.
