Competitor Analysis Framework

See what your competitors are doing — and find the gaps they're missing.

What This Does For You

There's a free tool called the Meta Ad Library. It shows you every ad any brand is running on Facebook and Instagram right now. It's one of the most valuable research tools in advertising and almost nobody uses it properly.

This framework teaches you how to use it — what to look for, how to organize what you find, and how to turn competitor intelligence into better ads for your own brand.

Important: The goal is intelligence, not imitation. We're not copying competitors — we're learning what's working in the market and finding the gaps they're missing.

How to Use This — Step by Step

1

Go to the Meta Ad Library 2 min setup

Go to facebook.com/ads/library on any computer. It's free, no account needed. Search your competitor's brand name. You'll see every ad they're currently running.

2

Identify their 3 most-used ads 10 min

Ads that have been running for 30+ days are working. Filter by "Active ads" and sort by start date. The ones running the longest are their best performers. Write down the first 3 seconds of each one.

3

Fill in the tracking template 15 min

Open TEMPLATES/competitor-tracker-blank.md. For each competitor ad, fill in: hook, claim, proof, format, and how long it's been running. Do this for 3–5 competitors.

4

Find what everyone is doing — and what nobody is doing 10 min

After filling in the tracker: what hooks do all your competitors use? What angles do they all take? That's the "crowded lane." What's nobody doing? What problem is nobody talking about? That's your gap. The examples folder shows how this works.

5

Write one creative idea based on the gap 10 min

Use the Video Script Framework (if you have it) to write one ad that takes the angle nobody else is using. Test it. That gap is often where the best ads live.

💡 The most valuable thing you'll find Every competitor says their product is "the best" or has "the most reviews." The gap is almost always emotional — nobody is talking to the customer's fear, frustration, or identity. That's where you win.

🔍 You don't need to be a strategist to use this. You just need 30 minutes and the ability to watch competitor ads with fresh eyes.
