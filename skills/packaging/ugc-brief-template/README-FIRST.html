UGC Brief Template

How to tell creators exactly what to make — so you get videos that actually sell.

First — What Is a UGC Brief?

UGC stands for "User-Generated Content." It means videos made by real people (not polished ad agencies) who talk about your product on camera. These ads work incredibly well because they feel authentic.

But here's the problem: if you just send a creator your product and say "make a video," you get something unusable. You need to tell them exactly what to say, what to show, and how to act. That's what this brief does.

Think of the brief as a script outline you hand to the creator. They fill it in with their own personality. You get the content you need without micromanaging.

How to Use This — Step by Step

1

Read one example brief 5 min

Open EXAMPLES/example-brief-skincare.md. Read it like you're the creator receiving it. Notice how specific it is — exact talking points, exact things to show, exact things to avoid.

2

Open your blank brief 2 min

Open TEMPLATES/ugc-brief-blank.md. This is your working document. It has every section you need with instructions in brackets.

3

Fill in sections 1–4 15 min

The brief has 6 sections. Sections 1–4 are the most important: product overview, what to say, what to show, and what NOT to do. Fill these in. Everything else is optional.

4

Send it to your creator 2 min

Copy and paste the brief into an email or message. Add a note: "Please confirm you've read all of this before filming." That one line saves you 90% of revision requests.

5

Review the video against the brief 10 min

When the creator delivers, open the brief and check each talking point. Did they cover everything? Did they avoid the "don'ts"? If something's missing, send them back to the brief — not a vague note.

💡 The most common mistake People write briefs that are too short. "Be authentic" is not a direction. Write every talking point as a complete sentence. If you could misinterpret it, so could the creator.

🎥 You don't need a creator network to use this. This brief works for professional creators, friends filming on iPhone, or yourself on camera. The brief is the same.
