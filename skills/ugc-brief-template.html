The Problem

Bad briefs are expensive. You pay twice -- for the content, then for the reshoot.

The brief that says "just be authentic and show the product" is not a brief. It's an invitation for generic content that looks like every other ad, costs you a reshoot, and still probably doesn't convert. Specific direction produces specific results.

  • Vague direction like "make it fun and relatable" gives creators nothing to work with -- they guess, you get mediocre content, and you spend another week requesting revisions
  • Missing talking points means creators freestyle brand messaging, often leaving out the key differentiator, social proof, or CTA that makes the ad work
  • No visual direction (lighting, setting, wardrobe) produces technically poor content that can't be used in paid ads even when the messaging is right
What's Inside

The brief that fails vs. the brief that works

The difference between a reshoot and a conversion-ready first delivery comes down to specificity. This template is built around specificity at every step.

Brief That Fails
  • "Just make it authentic"
  • "Show the product, be yourself"
  • "Make it fun and relatable"
  • "Keep it natural"
Brief That Works
  • 5 specific talking points in order
  • Exact hook requirement (first 3 seconds)
  • Setting, lighting, and wardrobe direction
  • What NOT to say or show
