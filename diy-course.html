Curriculum

4 modules. Start to finish.

Each module covers one phase of the skill-building process: understanding, extracting, building, and distributing. You'll leave with a finished skill, not just a plan.

Module 01

What Makes a Great Skill

Before you build anything, you need to understand what separates a skill people use from one that collects digital dust. Most fail at this first step.

  • Frameworks vs. templates vs. checklists -- and when to use each
  • The anatomy of a skill that actually gets used (and why most don't)
  • Common structure mistakes that make skills feel unusable
  • Skill audit: evaluate your existing knowledge assets
Module 02

Extract the Knowledge

The hardest part of building a skill is getting the knowledge out of your head. You'll learn two techniques that work even when you "don't know how to explain it."

  • The self-interview method: structured questions to surface your process
  • From case study to teachable system: reverse-engineering real work
  • How to document processes you've been doing on autopilot
  • When to ask for help vs. extract alone
Module 03

Build the Skill

Structure, writing, templates -- this is where the skill comes together. You'll build your first skill during this module, not after it.

  • The 5-part skill structure that works for any type of expertise
  • Writing for someone who doesn't have your context
  • Building the implementation guide (the most skipped piece)
  • Templates, examples, and visual aids -- what to include, what to cut
Module 04

Package and Distribute

A skill is only valuable if it reaches the right people. You'll learn how to format, price, and distribute your skill -- whether you want to sell it, share it, or use it internally.

  • Formats that work: Markdown, Notion, PDF, and web
  • How to price a skill (and what pricing signals to buyers)
  • Where to sell it: Gumroad, your own site, skill marketplaces
  • Using skills internally as training and onboarding tools
