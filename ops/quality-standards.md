## The Quality Checklist

Before any skill ships, it must pass:

### Content Quality
- [ ] Every claim has an example
- [ ] Every example is specific (not "a skincare brand" but "Glow Up's vitamin C campaign")
- [ ] No vague advice (not "make it engaging" but "use pattern interrupt in first 0.5 seconds")
- [ ] Frameworks are complete (start to finish, not partial)
- [ ] Immediately actionable (can be used within 24 hours of reading)

### Professional Standards
- [ ] Would Ogilvy approve the writing?
- [ ] Would a CMO feel confident sharing this with their team?
- [ ] Is it worth $39? (Would you pay $39 for this?)
- [ ] Is it worth $99 for the bundle?
- [ ] Would someone recommend this to a peer?

### Utility Check
- [ ] Can someone use this without asking questions?
- [ ] Are there clear next steps?
- [ ] Are the templates fill-in-the-blank ready?
- [ ] Is there a "quick start" for immediate use?
- [ ] Is there depth for advanced users?

## The Ogilvy Test

**Ask yourself:**

1. **Would Ogilvy read this and nod?**
   - Is it researched?
   - Is it specific?
   - Is it persuasive?

2. **Would a brand pay $50K for this advice if it was a consultation?**
   - Is the value there?
   - Is the expertise evident?
   - Are the frameworks proprietary?

3. **Would someone frame this and put it on their desk?**
   - Is it reference-worthy?
   - Is it beautifully designed?
   - Is it something they'd return to again and again?

4. **If this was the only thing you were known for, would you be proud?**
   - Is it your best work?
   - Does it represent your capabilities?
   - Would you stake your reputation on it?

**Every skill must pass all 4 questions.**

## Examples of $10M-Quality Work

**Good:** "Use a strong hook in the first 3 seconds"
**Better:** "The hook must create a pattern interrupt in the first 0.5 seconds using one of these 5 formulas"
**Best:** "Here are 5 hook formulas with exact word-for-word examples from 8-figure brands, plus why each works psychologically"

**Good:** "Write a clear CTA"
**Better:** "Your CTA should be specific"
**Best:** "Use this exact CTA structure: [Action] + [Benefit] + [Risk Removal]. Here are 10 examples across different categories"

**Good:** "Analyze your competitors"
**Better:** "Check their Meta Ad Library"
**Best:** "Use this 4-quadrant competitive matrix to identify gaps. Here's how to read between the lines of what they're testing"

## Final Polish Checklist

Before launch, review every skill:

**Writing:**
- [ ] No passive voice

- [ ] Metrics included where available

**Value:**
- [ ] Could someone charge $500 for this as a consultation?

- [ ] Would someone buy this again?
- [ ] Would they recommend it?
