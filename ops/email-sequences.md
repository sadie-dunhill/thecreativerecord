### Email 3: Deep Dive (Day 4)
**Subject:** The 30-second script structure that wins

Hi [First Name],

Today I want to share the exact 30-second video script structure used by 8-figure DTC brands.

**The 5-Beat Structure:**

**Beat 1: The Hook (0-3 seconds)**
Stop the scroll. Create pattern interrupt.

Example: "I used to spend $300 on skincare that didn't work. Then a dermatologist told me the truth."

**Beat 2: The Setup (3-8 seconds)**
Establish context. Make the problem relatable.

Example: "For years, I was layering 7 products every night. My bathroom looked like a Sephora. And my skin? Still dry."

**Beat 3: The Discovery (8-15 seconds)**
Introduce your solution. Build credibility.

Example: "She told me: 'You're overwhelming your skin barrier.' One product. Hyaluronic acid plus ceramides. That's it."

**Beat 4: The Proof (15-25 seconds)**
Demonstrate results. Overcome objections.

Example: "Two weeks in, my skin felt different. Balanced. I stopped using everything else. Saved 20 minutes every night."

**Beat 5: The CTA (25-30 seconds)**
Direct clear action. Remove friction.

Example: "Try the barrier repair serum. 60-day guarantee. Link in bio for 20% off."

**Total: 30 seconds**

This is the exact framework in the Video Script Framework skill. It includes 5 complete examples across different categories (skincare, supplements, fashion, home, fitness) plus a blank template to fill in.

Get the full framework here: thecreativerecord.com/skills

Talk soon,
Sadie

P.S. This structure works because it follows how people actually make decisions: attention → interest → desire → action.

## Email Best Practices

**Subject Lines:**
- Keep under 50 characters
- Front-load the value
- Use personalization [First Name]
- Test curiosity vs. direct

**Send Times:**
- Tuesday-Thursday perform best
- 9am-11am EST optimal
- Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons

**Length:**
- Welcome emails: Short (100-200 words)
- Educational emails: Medium (300-500 words)
- Story emails: Longer (500-800 words)

**CTAs:**
- One clear action per email
- Button for primary CTA
- Text link for secondary
- Make it obvious what to do
