**Document Owner:** Sadie Dunhill  
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026  
**Site:** thecreativerecord.com  
**Target Audience:** True small business owners -- local, service, solopreneur, small ecommerce  
**NOT targeting:** Enterprise, big DTC, Fortune 500  

---

## Executive Summary

Most marketing content talks to marketers. This plan targets the actual business owner -- the plumber who needs to run their first Facebook ad, the consultant who wants leads without hiring an agency, the restaurant owner who's heard "you need to be on social" but doesn't know where to start.

The opportunity: WordStream is built for agencies. HubSpot is built for marketing teams. Facebook Business is dry and corporate. The gap is practical, plain-English, actionable content written directly to the person running their own business and their own ads.

The Creative Record has a voice advantage here. Goodo's frameworks are powerful -- they just need to be translated down to SMB scale. The same Schwartz stages that help DTC brands can help a dentist write a Facebook ad. The same hook formulas that work for $50K/mo ad accounts work at $500/mo.

**12-Week North Star (SMB Track):**
- 300+ organic sessions/month from SMB-specific content by Week 12
- 3 cornerstone SMB articles ranking top 10 for target keywords
- Lead magnet converting at 5%+ from SMB organic traffic
- Email list growing 30+ SMB subscribers/week from organic

## Part 2: Content Strategy

### 2.1 SMB Positioning Guide

**The core tension SMB owners feel:**
They know they need marketing. They don't have a marketing team. They've heard agencies charge too much. They've tried to run ads themselves and lost money. They feel like all the marketing content is written for someone else -- bigger companies, different problems.

**What they actually need:**
- Someone to talk to them like they're smart, even if they're new to marketing
- Step-by-step, not just strategy
- Real examples from businesses like theirs
- Something they can act on today, not someday

**Positioning statement:**
> The Creative Record is where small business owners learn to make ads that work -- without an agency, without a big budget, and without a marketing degree. We translate the same frameworks used by 8-figure brands into tools that work at your scale.

**Messaging pillars:**

| Pillar | What It Means | Example Line |
|---|---|---|
| Built for your size | Not enterprise advice shrunk down -- frameworks designed for small accounts | "These strategies were tested on $500/mo budgets, not $50K/mo." |
| No agency required | You can do this yourself -- we'll show you how | "The creative director is you. Here's your playbook." |
| Same frameworks, smaller scale | Schwartz, Ogilvy, and direct response still work at your budget | "The same principles that built billion-dollar brands work at your scale." |
| Practical, not theoretical | Every piece of content has a template or action step | "Copy this exact structure for your next ad." |

**Tone guidelines for SMB content:**
- Open with the problem, not the concept ("You're not bad at marketing. Your ads just need a better hook.")
- Use "you" and "your business" liberally
- Avoid jargon unless you explain it immediately (ROAS = your return on ad spend -- how many dollars you get back for every dollar you put in)
- Include a "TL;DR" or "Quick Answer" box at the top of every post
- End with a single next action, not a list of 10 things

### 2.2 Content Pillars for SMB

**Pillar 1: Getting Started with Facebook Ads**
The biggest audience: people who have never run ads and are scared. Every piece in this pillar should feel like a patient explanation from someone who's done it 100 times.

Key subtopics:
- What Facebook ads actually are and how they work
- Setting up your first ad account
- Choosing the right objective (leads vs. sales vs. traffic)
- How much to spend when you're starting
- Reading your results without getting overwhelmed

**Pillar 2: Writing Ad Copy**
The most-Googled skill gap. SMBs often have a product and a budget but can't write copy that converts.

Key subtopics:
- The 3-part ad formula (hook + body + CTA)
- Writing for your customer's awareness stage
- Templates for common SMB ad types (lead gen, local, ecommerce)
- Headlines that stop the scroll
- What to say when you don't know what to say

**Pillar 3: Creative on a Budget**
SMBs can't afford a production house. This pillar teaches them to make good creative themselves or with a phone.

Key subtopics:
- iPhone video ads that don't look DIY
- Free tools for making static ads
- How to brief a freelance designer or videographer
- UGC without an influencer budget
- When to invest in professional creative vs. DIY

**Pillar 4: Local and Service Business Marketing**
The underserved vertical. Almost no one writes specifically for plumbers, accountants, coaches, and local shops.

Key subtopics:
- Facebook ads for location-based businesses
- Lead gen ads for service businesses
- Seasonal campaigns for local businesses
- Getting reviews to use in ads
- Competing with franchises and big brands locally

**Pillar 5: Testing and Optimization**
What happens after you launch. SMBs often set it and forget it or panic and kill ads too early.

Key subtopics:
- When to kill an ad (and when to give it more time)
- Simple A/B testing on a small budget
- Reading your metrics without a marketing degree
- Creative fatigue -- what it is and how to fix it
- Scaling from $500/mo to $2,000/mo

### 2.3 Content Depth Standards for SMB Audience

**Word count targets:**
- Pillar pages: 3,500--5,000 words (comprehensive but skimmable)
- How-to guides: 1,500--2,500 words (step-by-step, numbered)
- Quick answers / FAQ posts: 800--1,200 words (direct, fast)
- Template posts: 1,000--1,500 words + actual templates

**Structure requirements for every SMB post:**
1. TL;DR box at the top (3-5 bullet summary)
2. Problem-first opening paragraph (name the pain)
3. Numbered steps or clear section headers
4. At least one real example from a small business context
5. Template or swipe file the reader can use immediately
6. Single call-to-action at the end (not multiple competing ones)

**Things to include that competitors don't:**
- Dollar amounts, not percentages ("Start with $15/day" not "start with a small budget")
- Specific platforms and settings (Meta Ads Manager, not "your ad platform")
- Screenshots where relevant
- "What this looks like in practice" examples with fictional SMB scenarios
- "Common mistake" callout boxes

## Part 3: Local SEO Tactics

### 3.1 Location + Service Keyword Strategy

Even though The Creative Record is a national/digital publication, SMB searchers often add location modifiers. The strategy is to rank for the informational layer of those searches.

**Location-modified content opportunities:**

| Content Angle | Example Title | Search Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| City-specific guides | "Facebook Ads for Small Business in [City]" | Lower volume, higher intent |
| State-level guides | "Best Marketing Strategies for Texas Small Businesses" | Medium volume |
| Regional guidance | "Marketing for Small Business in the Pacific Northwest" | Low but qualified |
| "Near me" adjacent | "How to Target Local Customers with Facebook Ads" | Addresses the intent |

**Recommended approach:** Rather than building dozens of location pages (which can feel thin), build highly specific how-to content that addresses the local marketing use case. A post titled "How to Target Customers Within 5 Miles of Your Business on Facebook" captures the local intent without needing a city-specific page.

### 3.2 Industry + Vertical Landing Pages

One page per major SMB vertical. Not a thin directory page -- a real resource.

**Recommended verticals (ranked by keyword opportunity):**

### 3.3 Schema and Technical SEO for SMB Content

**Schema types to implement:**
- `HowTo` schema on all step-by-step posts (boosts featured snippet eligibility)
- `FAQPage` schema on question-based posts
- `Article` schema on all blog posts with `datePublished` and `dateModified`
- `BreadcrumbList` schema across all content

**Meta description templates for SMB content:**

For how-to posts:
> Learn how to [action] for your small business in [X] simple steps. Real examples, templates you can copy, and no marketing degree required.

For pillar pages:
> The complete guide to [topic] for small business owners. Covers [subtopic 1], [subtopic 2], and [subtopic 3] -- with templates and examples.

For question posts:
> [Direct answer to question] -- here's the honest breakdown for small business owners who want results without wasting budget.

## Part 6: Quick Wins (First 30 Days)

1. **Publish "Small Business Advertising 101" pillar page.** This is the hub everything links to. It signals to Google that this site has authority on the topic. Target 4,000+ words with full keyword optimization.

2. **Build and distribute the lead magnet.** Five ad templates as a PDF. Put it on every relevant post. This converts browsers into subscribers.

3. **Get listed in SMB resource directories.** SCORE.org, SBA resource pages, SmallBizTrends. Reach out with a genuine resource offer. These are free, high-authority backlinks.

4. **Publish 5 long-tail how-to posts.** Pick the 5 lowest-difficulty question keywords above and answer them better than anyone else. "How much should a small business spend on facebook ads" can rank in 30-60 days with the right structure.

5. **Add FAQ schema to all SMB content.** The fastest path to featured snippets. Every post should have 3-5 questions in FAQ schema.
